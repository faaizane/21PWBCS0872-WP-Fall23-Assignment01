Discovering Shigar Valley: A Comprehensive Guide to Pakistan's Hidden Gem

Nestled in the heart of Pakistan's Gilgit-Baltistan region, Shigar Valley is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. This picturesque valley is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. Whether you're an adventure enthusiast, a history buff, or a nature lover, Shigar Valley has something to offer for everyone. Here's your comprehensive guide to making the most of your visit to this enchanting destination.

Route

Shigar Valley is accessible by road from Skardu, the nearest major town, and the Skardu Airport. Skardu Airport is well-connected to Islamabad and Gilgit, making it relatively convenient to reach. From Skardu, a scenic 30-minute drive will take you to the heart of Shigar Valley.

Tourist Spots

1. Shigar Fort

Shigar Fort, also known as Fong Khar, is a historic gem of Baltistan. It has been beautifully restored and converted into a boutique hotel. Explore the fort's intricately decorated rooms, stunning gardens, and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

2. Deosai National Park

Known as the "Land of Giants," Deosai National Park offers a high-altitude wilderness experience. It's a great spot for wildlife enthusiasts and birdwatchers. During summer, the park transforms into a carpet of wildflowers, while in winter, it becomes a serene snowscape.

3. Shangrila Resort

Shangrila Resort, often referred to as "Heaven on Earth," is located near Shangrila Lake. Enjoy the pristine lake, take a ride on a traditional boat, and indulge in local cuisine at the resort's restaurant.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Shigar Valley is during the summer months, from May to September, when the weather is pleasant, and most of the activities and treks are accessible. However, for those seeking a different experience, winter (October to April) offers a unique charm with snow-covered landscapes.

Unveiling Hunza Valley: Your Definitive Travel Guide to the Jewel of Northern Pakistan

Hunza Valley is a stunning and remote region in the Gilgit-Baltistan area of Pakistan. It's known for its breathtaking landscapes, rich culture, and the warmth of its people. This complete guide will help you plan your trip to Hunza Valley, including information on how to get there, things to do, where to stay, and more.

Route

By Air: The nearest airport to Hunza Valley is Gilgit Airport. You can reach Gilgit by air from Islamabad, Pakistan.

By Road: Most travelers prefer to drive to Hunza Valley. You can rent a car or take a bus from Islamabad or Skardu, depending on your starting point..

Tourist Spots

1. Attabad Lake:

This turquoise lake was formed after a massive landslide in 2010 and is now a popular destination for boat rides.

2. Karimabad

The main town in Hunza, where you can explore Baltit Fort, Altit Fort, and the local market.

3. Hiking and Trekking

Hunza offers several trekking options, such as the Rakaposhi Base Camp, Ultar Sar Trek, and more.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Hunza Valley is from May to October when the weather is pleasant, and most attractions are accessible
